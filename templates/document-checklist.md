# Document Checklist Templates

These are static fallback checklists used when the AI is unavailable or returns LOW confidence.
The AI in the immigration intake workflow generates a case-specific checklist dynamically —
these templates are for reference and manual intake.

---

## H-1B Specialty Occupation (Cap-Subject)

1. Signed retainer agreement
2. Copy of passport (all pages)
3. All previous US visas and I-94 records
4. Current résumé / CV
5. Educational credentials (degrees, transcripts, diplomas)
6. Any prior H-1B petitions or approvals (I-797 notices)
7. Employer support letter (HR or hiring manager)
8. Job description / position description from employer
9. Employer's most recent IRS Form 941 (payroll)
10. Employer's most recent annual report or Form 10-K (if public)
11. Specialty occupation analysis (prepared by attorney)
12. Labor Condition Application (LCA) — filed by employer via FLAG system

---

## EB-2 National Interest Waiver (NIW)

1. Signed retainer agreement
2. Copy of passport
3. All previous US visas and I-94 records
4. Current résumé / CV (detailed, academic format)
5. All academic degrees and transcripts
6. List of publications (with citation counts)
7. Letters of recommendation (minimum 6 — mix of independent and collaborating experts)
8. Evidence of peer review work (journal names, number of reviews)
9. Evidence of media coverage or invited talks
10. Evidence of membership in professional associations
11. Evidence of original contributions to the field
12. Personal statement / cover letter

---

## Family-Based: Immediate Relative Spouse (IR-1/CR-1)

1. Signed retainer agreement
2. Petitioner's proof of US citizenship (passport, birth certificate, or naturalization certificate)
3. Beneficiary's valid passport
4. Marriage certificate (with certified translation if not in English)
5. Proof of dissolution of any prior marriages (divorce decrees)
6. Joint evidence of bona fide marriage (photos, joint accounts, lease/deed, communications)
7. Petitioner's most recent 3 years of tax returns (or IRS transcript)
8. I-864 Affidavit of Support (with supporting pay stubs and W-2s)
9. Police clearance certificates (beneficiary, from each country of residence)
10. Medical exam (Form I-693 or DS-2053 depending on pathway)
11. Two passport-style photos per person

---

## Naturalization (N-400)

1. Signed retainer agreement
2. Copy of Green Card (front and back)
3. Copy of passport (all pages)
4. All US tax returns for past 5 years (or 3 years if married to USC)
5. Proof of continuous residence (rent receipts, utility bills, bank statements)
6. Travel history outside the US for past 5 years (all trips over 24 hours)
7. Marriage certificate (if married)
8. Divorce decrees (for all prior marriages)
9. Any criminal history disclosure (arrests, citations, convictions)
10. Selective Service registration confirmation (males 18-26 at any time)

---

## Asylum (Affirmative)

1. Signed retainer agreement
2. Form I-589 (prepared with attorney)
3. Passport and any travel documents
4. Any prior immigration court notices or orders
5. Evidence of persecution (police reports, medical records, news articles, photos)
6. Witness statements
7. Country condition evidence (US State Dept reports, NGO reports, news)
8. Personal declaration (prepared with attorney — detailed account of persecution)
9. Any prior asylum filings or decisions in any country
